# Your starter checklist

You've reached the end. This last "lesson" isn't a lesson at all — it's a **checklist of everything the course taught you to do**, in one place. Print it (the **Print** button is right there in the sidebar), tick off what you've actually done with your own hands, and you'll have a clear picture of where you stand — and a tidy record of what you accomplished.

> **Tip:** the boxes below print as real, tickable boxes. This page makes a great one-page summary to keep, or to show someone what you built.

## The core skills

These are the six things that make you a competent WolfStack operator. If you can tick all of these, you're genuinely running your own infrastructure.

- [ ] I can find my server in the sidebar and look at what's on it
- [ ] I installed an app from the **App Store** and opened it in my browser
- [ ] I built a container myself (**Docker** or **LXC**) from scratch
- [ ] I opened a **terminal** — on a server and inside a container
- [ ] I took a **backup**, and I set up an **automatic** one
- [ ] I did a **test restore** and confirmed it actually came back
- [ ] I know the **Issues** page is my 30-second daily health check

## Locking it down

Security isn't optional once a machine is reachable. Tick these off and you've shut the door on the overwhelming majority of attacks.

- [ ] I set a **strong, unique password** on my login
- [ ] I turned on **2FA** and/or added a **passkey**
- [ ] I pushed a **brute-force lockout policy** — and added my own IP to **Trusted IPs** first
- [ ] I checked **Internet-exposed services** and closed anything red I didn't mean to expose
- [ ] I turned on **NMAP Protection** and **Threat Intel**

## Staying ahead of trouble

- [ ] I set up **alerts** to one channel I actually check (Discord / Slack / Telegram)
- [ ] I clicked **Send Test Alert** and the message arrived
- [ ] *(Optional)* I put up a public **status page**
- [ ] *(Optional)* I put an app on a **real domain with HTTPS**

## What to do next

You don't have to do everything at once — that was never the point. Pick whatever's true for you:

1. **Anything unticked above?** Go back to that lesson (the compass, bottom-left) and do it for real. Reading isn't doing.
2. **All ticked?** You've finished the foundations. When you next have a *specific* need — "I want my two sites' servers to talk," "I want to automate cleanup" — open that one door (see **The map of everything else**) and learn it the same way: one screen, one task.
3. **Stuck on anything?** Re-read **How to get unstuck** — revisit a lesson, ask the built-in AI, check Issues, then the docs and community.
